## üá©üáø Algeria - Day 39/100 of One Country a Day Project

Algeria stands as North Africa's largest nation ‚Äî a country of vast desert landscapes and Mediterranean coastlines, abundant hydrocarbon wealth and economic diversification challenges, colonial trauma and post-independence identity. As Africa's largest country by land area and one of the continent's top economies, Algeria possesses the world's tenth-largest proven natural gas reserves and sixteenth-largest oil reserves. Yet decades of resource dependency, political transitions, and regional instability have shaped a complex development trajectory. From the 1962 War of Independence that ended 132 years of French colonial rule to the 1990s civil conflict, from the strategic Mediterranean position that made it a key energy supplier to Europe to the ongoing challenges of youth unemployment and economic reform, Algeria's story is one of transformation: rich natural endowments seeking pathways beyond hydrocarbons. Today, we examine this North African nation that bridges the Sahara and the Mediterranean, balancing between resource wealth and economic modernization.
